Galileo Galilei

"The man who create the first movement theory"

Here is a summary of the most important aspects:

  • Finding his Vocation

    1583- His father wanted him to become in medicine. Galileo specialized in math, with help from Ricc, a family's friend. Ricci was used to practice experimental theory back in those days. He was attracted by Euclides work, and with no interest in medicine, he re-oriented his studies to math. Since then, he felt a Pitagora's follower. As a student he was able to discover the law of isochrony of pendulums. After two years, he returns to his home without a diploma, but with the greatest knowledge and cientific curiosity

  • Invention of the telescope

    1609- He increases his curiosity when one of his oldest students confirms to him the existence of a telescope capable to see objects far away. This device was built in Netherlands by a glasses maker, Hans Lippershey. Motivated, he starts to build his own telescope. The opposite to the telescope from Netherlands, this one did not affect objects and increases 6 times it size. On August 21st, he just had finished his second telescope, that could increase 8 or 9 times the size of an object. He made a demonstration in Venecia, on the Campanile, San Marcos square. The crowd was exited, infront of them Murano, at 2 and a half miles from them, they were able to see it just at 300 meters.

  • Moon Watching

    1610- During autuum on 1610, Galileo continued developing his Telescope. In November, he made a device that couls increase the size 20 times and used it to watch the sky. He was able to observe the moon phases, and discovered this object was a sphere. He was able to see and transitory light/shadow that was not regular.

  • Proofs of the heliocentric system presented by Galileo

    According to Bertrand Russel, the conflict between Galileo and the Catholic Church was a rasoning conflict. The induction was base in the observation of reality, from the scientist, and what was written according the holly bible. Within all the proofs presente by Galileo, we have the following:

    1. Mountains on the Moon
    2. New Stars
    3. Sun's Spots
    4. Jupiter's Satellites
    5. Venus's Phases
    6. Change of tides

  • Last Years

    1638- Galileo remains confined at his home, receiving some visitors. This allows him to keep working on his theories In Januaty 2nd, 1638, Galileo looses his sight. He dies when he was 77 years old.

Tribute

The scientific community has expressed during all these years their position in regards to Galileo and the Catholic Church. The church in Galileo's time, was attended to use the reason than Galileo, he did not take into considetation the impact this would create in the ethical and social matters. The sentence for Galileo was fair just for political matters. Inside the scientific community these days, Galileo is considered the father of a lot of theories that have been modeling what science is right now.
